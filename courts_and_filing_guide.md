## STEP-BY-STEP FILING PROCEDURE

### STAGE 1: REFERENCE APPLICATION (Section 18)

#### Step 1: Prepare Documentation
- Draft reference application as per format
- Collect all supporting documents
- Arrange comparable sale deeds and judgments
- Get valuation report (optional but helpful)

#### Step 2: File Before Collector/SLAO
**Where:** Office of the Collector/SLAO who passed the Award  
**When:** Within limitation period (6 weeks/6 months as applicable)  
**Documents to submit:**
1. Reference Application in triplicate
2. All supporting documents (certified copies)
3. Court fee stamp/challan as per State rules
4. Vakalatnama (if filed through advocate)

#### Step 3: Acknowledgment
- Obtain dated acknowledgment/receipt
- Note the Application Number assigned
- Retain copies for record

#### Step 4: Collector's Action
- Collector examines the application
- If in order, forwards to District Court for reference
- State government will be made party (usually represented by Govt. Pleader)

#### Step 5: District Court Proceedings
**Court:** Principal District Judge / District Court  
**Case Type:** Land Acquisition Case (LAC) / Reference Case  
**Procedure:**
1. Court registers the reference
2. Issues notice to all parties
3. Both sides file written statements
4. Evidence stage: Examination of witnesses, documents
5. Arguments by both sides
6. Judgment and Award

**Timeline:** Typically 1-3 years (varies by court workload)

---

### STAGE 2: FIRST APPEAL TO HIGH COURT (Section 54)

#### Step 1: Obtain Certified Copy
**From:** District Court that passed the Reference Judgment  
**Timeline:** Apply immediately after judgment (within 7 days)  
**Cost:** As per Court fee rules  
**Collection:** Usually takes 15-30 days

#### Step 2: Draft Memorandum of Appeal
- As per High Court Rules
- Must contain grounds of appeal
- Valuation for court fee purposes
- List of documents

#### Step 3: Arrange Court Fees
**Type:** Ad valorem (based on disputed compensation amount)  
**Calculation:** As per Court Fees Act of the State  
**Example:** If seeking enhancement from Rs. 10 lakhs to Rs. 20 lakhs, court fee calculated on Rs. 10 lakhs difference

#### Step 4: Filing at High Court
**Where:** Filing Counter / e-Filing portal of concerned High Court  
**Mode:**
- **Physical filing:** At designated counter with hard copies
- **e-Filing:** Through High Court's online portal (if available)

**Documents Required:**
1. Memorandum of Appeal (multiple copies as per rules)
2. Vakalatnama
3. Certified copy of impugned judgment
4. Certified copies of all documents relied upon
5. Index of documents
6. Affidavit of service/notice
7. Court fee paid receipt
8. Notarized translations (if documents in regional language and HC requires English)

#### Step 5: Defect Memo
- Court registry checks the appeal
- Issues defect memo if any deficiency
- Defects must be cured within prescribed time (usually 30-45 days)

#### Step 6: Case Number Allotted
- Upon accepting the appeal, case number is allotted
- First Appeal No. _____ of 20___

#### Step 7: Listing and Hearing
**Admission Stage:**
- First listed for admission
- Court examines if substantial question of law/fact
- May issue notice to respondents

**Final Hearing:**
- After admission, listed for final arguments
- Both sides argue on merits
- Court may call for additional evidence/documents

**Judgment:**
- Court passes final judgment
- Enhances / reduces / maintains compensation
- Decision is binding

**Timeline:** 2-5 years (varies by High Court and case complexity)

---

### STAGE 3: SUPREME COURT (SLP)

#### When to Approach:
- Against final judgment of High Court
- Only if substantial question of law of general importance
- Or gross perversity/miscarriage of justice

#### Procedure:
1. File Special Leave Petition (SLP) under Article 136
2. Within limitation period (usually 90 days from HC judgment)
3. Supreme Court may grant or refuse leave
4. If leave granted, appeal heard on merits

**Location:** Supreme Court of India, New Delhi  
**Timeline:** Several years typically

## IMPORTANT FILING TIMELINES

| Stage | Limitation Period | Condonable? |
|-------|------------------|-------------|
| **Reference Application (Section 18)** | 6 weeks (if present at award) OR 6 weeks from Section 12(2) notice OR 6 months from award date | YES (under Section 5, Limitation Act) |
| **First Appeal (Section 54)** | 60-90 days from receipt of certified copy (check local HC rules) | YES (under Section 5, Limitation Act) |
| **SLP to Supreme Court** | 90 days from High Court judgment | YES (under Article 136) |
| **Application for Certified Copy** | Apply within 7 days of judgment (recommended) | Not applicable |
| **Curing Defects** | 30-45 days from defect memo (as per HC rules) | May be extended |

## COURT FEE STRUCTURE (GENERAL GUIDE)

### Reference Application (Section 18):
- **Nominal fee:** Rs. 50 to Rs. 500 (varies by State)
- Some states may have ad valorem fee

### First Appeal to High Court (Section 54):
- **Ad valorem:** Based on disputed compensation amount
- **Formula:** Usually 7.5% to 10% of disputed value (subject to ceiling)
- **Example:** Disputed amount Rs. 10 lakhs ‚Üí Court fee Rs. 75,000 (at 7.5%)
- **Ceiling:** Maximum court fee capped (varies: Rs. 5 lakhs to Rs. 25 lakhs)

### Condonation of Delay Application:
- **Fixed fee:** Rs. 100 to Rs. 500 (varies)

### Certified Copy Fees:
- **Per page:** Rs. 5 to Rs. 20 per page
- **Urgent/Tatkal:** Double or triple rates

**Note:** Exact fees vary by State. Check State-specific Court Fees Act and High Court rules.

## PRACTICAL FILING TIPS

### ‚úÖ DO's:

1. **Act Immediately:**
   - Apply for certified copy within 7 days of judgment
   - File within limitation period
   - Don't wait till last day

2. **Documentation:**
   - Maintain chronological file of all documents
   - Get certified copies (not plain photocopies)
   - Organize with proper index

3. **Engage Qualified Lawyer:**
   - Land acquisition law is specialized
   - Local lawyer with High Court experience preferred
   - Check track record in similar cases

4. **Evidence Collection:**
   - Collect comparable sale deeds immediately
   - Get Collector's Guidelines/Jantri rates
   - Take photographs of land showing advantages
   - Engage registered valuer if possible

5. **Follow Court Procedures:**
   - Strictly follow High Court rules for format
   - File requisite number of copies
   - Pay correct court fee (calculation errors cause delay)

6. **E-Filing:**
   - Use e-filing if available (faster, transparent)
   - Keep digital copies of all documents
   - Check status online regularly

7. **Attend Hearings:**
   - Don't remain absent on hearing dates
   - Keep in touch with your lawyer
   - Respond to court directions promptly

### ‚ùå DON'Ts:

1. **Don't Delay:**
   - Filing beyond limitation requires condonation
   - Long delays may be rejected
   - Each day counts

2. **Don't Submit Uncertified Copies:**
   - Courts reject plain photocopies
   - All documents must be certified by issuing authority

3. **Don't Ignore Defect Memo:**
   - Curing defects is mandatory
   - Failure to cure = appeal may be dismissed

4. **Don't File Incomplete Appeal:**
   - Missing documents = defects
   - Wrong court fee = rejection

5. **Don't Rely on Old Comparables:**
   - Sales more than 2-3 years old have less value
   - Courts prefer recent transactions near notification date

6. **Don't Accept Compensation Without "Under Protest" Statement:**
   - Acceptance without protest may bar reference
   - Always state "under protest" in writing

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS (FAQs)

### Q1: Can I file reference application without a lawyer?
**A:** Yes, you can file in person. However, given the technical nature and importance, engaging a lawyer is highly recommended.

---

### Q2: What if I missed the limitation period?
**A:** File an application for condonation of delay under Section 5 of Limitation Act along with your reference/appeal. Court may condone if sufficient cause is shown.

---

### Q3: Is acceptance of compensation compulsory before filing reference?
**A:** No. But if you accept, do so "under protest" in writing to preserve your right to file reference.

---

### Q4: Can I get compensation pending appeal?
**A:** Yes. The compensation awarded by LAO can be withdrawn. Enhanced amount (if any) pending appeal can be claimed after favorable judgment.

---

### Q5: How long do these cases take?
**A:** 
- Reference Court: 1-3 years
- High Court Appeal: 2-5 years
- Supreme Court: 3-8 years
Total: Can take 6-15 years in some cases.

---

### Q6: What is the success rate?
**A:** Varies widely. Cases with strong comparable evidence and proper documentation have higher success rates (60-80%). Cases without good evidence may not succeed.

---

### Q7: Can government file appeal against enhancement?
**A:** Yes. Government can file appeal under Section 54 challenging the Reference Court's enhancement.

---

### Q8: Is personal hearing required?
**A:** Generally, your lawyer represents you. Personal appearance may be required:
- During evidence recording stage
- If court directs personal appearance

---

### Q9: What if land is in remote village but I live in city?
**A:** You can file through local lawyer having practice in the District Court / High Court having jurisdiction over that area. Coordination through email/phone is common.

---

### Q10: Can I withdraw the reference/appeal?
**A:** Yes, at any stage. Court will pass orders disposing of the case as withdrawn.
