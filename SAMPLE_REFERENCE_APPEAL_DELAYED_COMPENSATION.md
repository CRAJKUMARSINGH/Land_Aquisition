**a) Awarded Compensation is Arbitrary and Unreasonable**
- The LAO awarded Rs. ___ per acre, which is grossly inadequate
- Current market value in the area is Rs. ___ per acre (based on recent transactions)
- The award ignores the actual potential and location advantages of the land

**b) Comparable Sale Evidence**
- Sale deed dated [date] for adjacent land (Survey No. ___) at Rs. ___ per acre

- Registration documents showing market trends in the area

**c) Collector's Guidelines Ignored**
- District Collector's guidelines for [year] prescribe minimum rate of Rs. ___ per acre
- LAO failed to follow statutory guidelines
- No justification provided for deviation from guidelines

### **GROUND 2: ERRONEOUS DETERMINATION PROCESS**

**a) Failure to Consider Land Characteristics**
- Land abuts [road/highway] with excellent accessibility
- Land has [soil type/water source/other advantages]
- Land is suitable for [residential/commercial/industrial] development

**b) Ignoring Time Escalation Factor**
- Notification issued in [year], award passed in [year]
- Significant time gap warrants escalation in market value
- LAO failed to account for inflation and market appreciation

**c) Improper Classification of Land**
- Land classified as agricultural when suitable for development
- LAO ignored potential for non-agricultural use
- Higher compensation warranted for development potential

- Mandatory provision ignored by LAO

**b) Interest Under Section 28**

## **IMPORTANT LEGAL OBSERVATIONS FROM SUCCESSFUL CASES**

### **1. On Delay Condonation:**
- **Liberal Approach**: Courts adopt liberal approach for condonation of delay
- **Sufficient Cause**: "Sufficient cause" must be liberally construed
- **No Interest Condition**: Courts may condone delay with condition of no interest for delay period

### **2. On Market Value Enhancement:**
- **Parity Principle**: Same compensation for lands under same notification
- **Comparable Evidence**: Recent sale deeds are strong evidence of market value
- **Collector's Guidelines**: Must be followed unless justified otherwise

### **3. On Acceptance Under Protest:**
- **Preserves Rights**: Acceptance under protest does not bar reference
- **Section 31(2)**: Second proviso allows reference if amount received under protest
- **No Estoppel**: Filing Section 18 application manifests protest

### **4. On Limitation Period:**
- **6 Months from Knowledge**: Period runs from date of knowledge, not date of award
- **Actual Knowledge**: Knowledge must relate to essential contents of award
- **Constructive Knowledge**: Award must be communicated to affected party

**Tamil Nadu:** Madras High Court, Chennai/Madurai  
**Telangana:** Telangana High Court, Hyderabad  
**Gujarat:** Gujarat High Court, Ahmedabad  

### **Filing Tips:**
1. **Territorial Jurisdiction**: File in High Court having territorial jurisdiction over land location
2. **Circuit Benches**: Check if your state has circuit benches closer to your location
3. **E-Filing**: Most High Courts have e-filing portals available
4. **Court Fees**: Ad valorem fees (7.5%-10% of disputed value) for appeals
