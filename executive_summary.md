## üéØ KEY SUCCESS FACTORS IDENTIFIED

### 1. TIMING IS CRITICAL
‚úÖ File within limitation (preferably within 6 weeks)  
‚úÖ If delayed, show genuine cause (illness, poverty, lack of knowledge)  
‚úÖ Act immediately after receiving compensation/knowledge  
‚ùå Don't wait for other claimants' outcomes

### 2. PARITY PRINCIPLE = STRONGEST WEAPON
If you can find a judgment for:
- ‚úÖ Same notification/project
- ‚úÖ Same locality
- ‚úÖ Same acquiring authority
- ‚úÖ Similar or inferior land

**‚Üí You have 80-90% chance of equal/higher compensation**

**Example:** Anand case - Direct application of earlier judgment for same Malaprabha Project awarded Rs. 5.08 lakh/acre

### 3. COMPARABLE EVIDENCE TYPES (in order of strength)

**Tier 1 (Strongest):**
1. **Comparable Judgments** - Same locality, recent
2. **Collector's Guidelines/Jantri Rates** - Official government rates

**Tier 2 (Strong):**
3. **Registered Sale Deeds** - Within 1-2 years of notification, same village
4. **Expert Valuer Report** - By government-approved valuer

**Tier 3 (Supporting):**
5. Land advantages documentation (photos, location map)
6. Income potential proof

### 4. "UNDER PROTEST" IS MANDATORY
- ‚úÖ Accept compensation with written "under protest" statement
- ‚úÖ Or file reference immediately after acceptance = implied protest
- ‚ùå Never accept without protest if you intend to challenge

### 5. PROCEDURAL COMPLIANCE
- ‚úÖ Certified copies of all documents (not plain photocopies)
- ‚úÖ Proper court fee payment
- ‚úÖ File in correct court (territorial jurisdiction)
- ‚úÖ Respond to defect memos promptly

## üéì LEGAL PRINCIPLES EXTRACTED

### On Limitation:
1. **"Date of award" = Date of knowledge** (actual or constructive)
2. **6 months runs from knowledge**, not physical award date
3. Liberal interpretation favored to avoid discrimination
4. Notice under Section 12(2) must be accompanied by award copy

**Key Cases:**
- Bhagwan Das v. State of U.P. (2010) 3 SCC 545
- Premji Nathu v. State of Gujarat (2012) 5 SCC 250

### On Condonation of Delay:
1. **Liberal approach** when genuine cause shown
2. Financial hardship + no willful delay = usually condoned
3. But "fence-sitting" = rejected
4. Readiness to forego interest for delay period strengthens case

**Key Cases:**
- Collector v. Mst. Katiji (AIR 1987 SC 1353)
- K. Subbarayadu v. SDC (2017) 12 SCC 840

### On Acceptance Under Protest:
1. Filing Section 18 application = **implied protest**
2. Explicit "under protest" statement safer
3. Acceptance without protest bars reference (Section 31(2))

**Key Cases:**
- Chandra Bhan v. GDA (2015) 15 SCC 343
- Ajit Singh v. State of Punjab (2000) 8 SCC 471

### On Market Value Determination:
1. **Comparable sales** - most reliable (same locality, recent)
2. **Collector's guidelines** - valid evidence (Lal Chand case)
3. **Parity principle** - equal treatment for same notification
4. **Time escalation** - must be granted for time gap
